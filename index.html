Vinyl Records

Pressed To The Edge:

Why are independent labels being priced out of their own culture?

Big vs Small

It is nothing new for large corporations to be harming small businesses.

For centuries, large corporations have been destroying the uniqueness and wonders of them.

This is no different for the modern vinyl record industry and it is destroying the culture.

Independent Labels

They kept vinyl alive whilst major labels were cashing in on CDs, digital downloads and streaming.

Their genuine love for the format and music kept pressing plants from closing down in the 90s to early 2000s.

Why should their care for the culture and customers be destroyed by money hungry major labels?

Major Labels

They have seen the rise in popularity of vinyl due to independent labels and want to ca$h in.

Pressing thousands of globally popular records only because they will sell well, not for the love of the format and music.

They are swamping production lines and leaving the labels who kept it alive to lose out.

There is a problem.

What is the problem?

Independent labels that never stopped releasing music on vinyl and saved the production infrastructure from bankruptcy are now trapped in a highly competetive industry. The manufacturers are finding it extremely hard to keep up with demand, due to new pressure from major labels.

Major labels are buying their way back into the industry that they destroyed and are starving independent labels.

Longer waiting times for records are forcing independent labels to increase their prices to survive. This also means loyal and valuable customers who have contributed to the life of the record are being treated unfairly.

The higher prices and longer waiting times are casuing integral buyers to leave the market.

Today, if an independent label's record is successful, it is impossible to repress it quickly in order to keep up with demand.

This is not sustainable for independent labels.

We need to make a change.
